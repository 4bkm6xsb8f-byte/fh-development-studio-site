Brand Standards

Visual and voice rules for FH Development Studio.

This document captures the core brand decisions reflected in the site so future pages, apps, and materials stay consistent.

Document Purpose

This internal document defines the core visual, verbal, and interface rules for FH Development Studio. Use it as the source of truth for future websites, landing pages, app marketing, presentations, and product-facing brand work.

Brand Position

FH Development Studio should present as calm, capable, and practical. The brand is not flashy or overly technical. It communicates trust through clarity, restraint, and evidence of thoughtful execution.

  • Positioning: a focused mobile and web development partner for thoughtful, reliable software.
  • Primary traits: clear, polished, dependable, direct, and modern.
  • Avoid: hype language, cluttered layouts, trend-chasing visuals, or overly abstract messaging.

Core Voice

  • Clear, direct, and professional without sounding corporate or stiff.
  • Confident but understated, with an emphasis on reliability and usability.
  • Focused on practical outcomes, honest scope, and long-term maintainability.
  • Supportive and human, with enough warmth to feel approachable.

FH Development Studio currently uses three brand asset forms: the full logo, the icon mark, and a text workmark. Use the full logo when space allows, the icon mark when a compact symbol is needed, and the workmark for text-led internal or editorial layouts.

Logo Rules

  • Clear space: keep at least the height of the icon mark around all sides of the logo lockup.
  • Minimum size: do not use the full logo below 132px wide in digital layouts.
  • Do not: stretch, rotate, recolor arbitrarily, add shadows, or place the logo on visually noisy backgrounds.
  • Background preference: use clean light, soft blue, or dark blue surfaces with strong contrast.

Typography

The site uses a clean system sans-serif stack for consistency across Apple and web environments. Headlines should feel crisp and modern, while body text should stay comfortable and readable.

Standard body copy should remain calm, readable, and aligned to a comfortable measure.
  • Font family: `-apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "Segoe UI", Roboto, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif`
  • Weight system: 400 for body, 700-800 for headings, labels, and navigation.
  • Text colors: headings use `#0D3580`, primary body copy uses `#14315F`, supporting copy uses `#38527F`, and reverse text uses white on dark backgrounds.
  • Rule: do not introduce secondary display fonts without updating this guide.

Layout Rules

  • Keep core content aligned to the shared container width.
  • Use centered banner copy only when the entire banner composition is centered.
  • Cards, content sections, and footer elements should share the same left and right rhythm.
  • Avoid crowding text against dividers, card edges, or footer rule lines.
  • Container width: max 1180px with consistent page gutter behavior.
  • Default section padding: 56px top and bottom on desktop, 40px on mobile.

Accessibility and Contrast

  • Prefer dark blue text on white or mist backgrounds for long-form readability.
  • Reserve bright blue for accents, highlights, and gradients rather than dense text blocks.
  • Use green for action emphasis and positive signal states, not for long-form content.
  • When placing brand assets on dark backgrounds, verify edge clarity and adequate breathing room.

Imagery and Art Direction

  • Favor crisp, utility-driven imagery over generic stock metaphors.
  • Use subtle gradients, abstract light fields, or clean software-oriented compositions.
  • Keep visuals organized, confident, and spacious rather than busy or overly playful.

Do and Don’t

  • Do keep pages airy, aligned, and easy to scan.
  • Do use the existing blue-green palette and restrained gradient system.
  • Do keep copy focused on usefulness, trust, and polished execution.
  • Don’t mix unrelated font treatments or abrupt heading scales between pages.
  • Don’t let banners, cards, and footers drift out of alignment with the main content grid.
