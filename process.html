Process

A practical delivery process from scope to support.

FH Development Studio keeps projects moving by clarifying priorities early, making decisions visible, and keeping the work tied to usability, delivery confidence, and business value.

Overview

Enough structure to reduce risk without slowing the work down.

The process is designed for focused teams that want clear recommendations, accountable delivery, and a launch plan that makes sense for the product stage.

01

Discovery and Priorities

Define the problem, target users, delivery constraints, and the smallest launch that creates useful business value.

02

Design and Build

Translate priorities into interface decisions and implementation work that stays clean, testable, and understandable.

03

Launch and Improve

Prepare for release, tighten remaining details, respond to issues, and continue with measured post-launch iteration.

Detail

What the work typically includes.

Discovery and Scope

Early conversations focus on what needs to be built, what can wait, where the main delivery risks are, and what kind of first release will be most useful.

Interface Direction

Product flows, layouts, and content structure are shaped around clarity and usability so the interface supports the goal instead of getting in the way.

Implementation

Build decisions are made with maintainability in mind, especially where future updates, support needs, or post-launch iteration are likely.

QA and Launch Readiness

Before launch, the focus shifts to quality checks, edge cases, support readiness, and the details that influence whether the product feels finished.

Post-Launch Support

After release, the next step is usually a practical review of feedback, issues, and opportunities for improvement based on real usage.
